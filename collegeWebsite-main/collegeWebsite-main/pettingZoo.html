Goats: Mostly hay and grains, but they’ll try to eat your map if you aren't careful.
Sheep: High-quality grass, clover, and the occasional sheep-safe mineral lick.
Pigs: A mix of corn, soy, and all the "ugly" veggies that don't make it to the farm stand.
Chickens: Scratch grains, seeds, and any brave insects that wander into the coop.
Donkeys: Strictly high-fiber forage and straw (they get "pudgy" way too easily on rich grass).
Llamas: Pasture grass and their absolute favorite: carrots (cut into sticks, please!).
Cows: A simple diet of fresh pasture grass and fermented silage in the winter.
